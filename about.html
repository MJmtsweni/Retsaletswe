About us

Welcome, here is what we are about

To create quality and excellent theatre work to the people living in rural areas and provide high quality education using drama as a medium.

In order to achieve our vision, therefore, our mission is to make resources more accessible to the artists living in rural areas and provide them with high education and artistic aid.

     
  • Developing theatre audience in rural areas.
  • Unearthing talent in rural areas.
  • Closing the gap between formally trained Actors, Directors, Writer and informally trained Artists living in the rural areas.
  • Educating, creating platforms for debates and developing national cohesion through the use of performing arts.
  • To promote education through music, dance and drama.
  • To unearth raw talent through arts, dance and drama.
  • To encourage maximum youth participation through dance, and drama.
  • We treat all people with respect and act with honesty and integrity in all we do.
  • We do not compromise the quality of our work.
  • We respect all our stakeholders and Their needs.
  • We value our clients and aim to educate and entertain them with our offerings and service.

Retsaletswe House Production prides itself in fighting against political social and economic injusticies. Our prime objective is to make our people aware of Their political, social and economic oppression through the work of art.


Leaders as well as the community need to identify themselves through the work of art that poeple can reflect on or in Their work and to understand the peole's frustrations, their needs or his or her contribution either negative or positive. South African communities are the same, meaning the same problems one is subjected to, it is highly possible that the next community might be going through the same predicament.

Meet our founders

Retsaletswe House Production, GaboDArts, and Pharatlhatlhe Theatre

Thabang Gabogope

Thabang Gabogope is a 29-year Theatre-Maker, Performer, Writer, Voice, and Theatre history Teacher. He studied Drama at The Market Theatre Laboratory. He is holding Bachelor of Arts Honours in Applied Drama in the field of Drama in Education at Wits University. He then went to study online short-playwritingg Course with Greg Homann. He was recently select to take part in Nedbank program ACT Thuthukisani programme Masterclasses and mentorship training. He is an award Bursary recipient from Artinsideout from America for Theatre-Making during his second year of study at the Market Theatre, NAF Award-winning best student Marose Co-director mentored by Greg Homann. Wrote and Directed Kgaube premiered at Zwakala Art Festival in 2019 then went to play at the South African State Theatre under Youth Expressions Festival in 2021. He is currently working on the new production in the North-west province called: Going to fetch Kathy, wrote and Directed by him made possible by Goethe-institute Johannesburg.


On his early stage in Theatre, He joined community groups, and his performance experience includes Bana pele, Directed by Mbole Moncho, performed at Community Art Festival at The Market Theatre. The tragedy of the Hopers, Directed by Onkaetse Maclean performed at Community Art Festival and National Art Festival. The last Testament Directed by Onkaetse Maclean Performed at Community Art Festival and National Art Festival. The Naked Dreams Directed by Tebogo Ramose, Performed at North West Cultural Calabash to mention a few.


His student Production at The Market Theatre Laboratory includes 21 Wander Directed by Mwenya Khabwe, The Oresteia Revised Directed by Dorothy-Ann Gould, African day Spectacle Directed by Omphile Molusi, and The saga of a City hermit Directed by Monageng “Vice” Motshabi. He co-directed Etshomo performed at Inner City High School Drama Festival at Hillbrow Theatre and Co-directed Marose which won the Best Student Production at National Art Festival. He wrote and Directed Kgaube performed at Zwakala Festival. He performed on the radio play “the day we pressed pause” by Sthe, he also performed the trial of Dedan Kimathi Directed by Billy Edward Langa.
